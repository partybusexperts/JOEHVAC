AC Not Cooling - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/ac-not-cooling or ACNotCooling.com
Problem Site #1

Overview

"AC not cooling" is the classic summer panic search. This problem site walks through the usual suspects--filters, coils, refrigerant, and airflow--using plain English, polls, and quick tools.

Trivia

  • Many systems can lose 5-10% efficiency per year if filters are never changed.
  • A slightly frozen coil can look harmless at first...but it often means airflow or refrigerant trouble.
  • Running an AC that isn't cooling for hours just to "see if it catches up" usually makes the problem worse.

Fast Facts

  • Most "not cooling" calls start with a dirty filter or blocked return vent.
  • Low refrigerant is a symptom, not a plan--if it's low, it probably leaked somewhere.
  • Closing too many supply vents can starve your system of airflow.

Poll Questions (on the micro-site)

  • How long has your AC been blowing warm air?
  • Is the airflow strong or weak from your vents?
  • Have you changed your filter in the past 60 days?

Furnace Not Heating - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/furnace-not-heating or FurnaceNotHeating.com
Problem Site #2

Overview

When the furnace blows cold air or shuts off after a few minutes, comfort drops fast. This site explains pilot lights, ignition sequences, and safety switches without getting overly technical.

Trivia

  • Some modern furnaces can try to ignite three times and then lock out for safety.
  • A furnace that "short cycles" can waste more gas than one that runs steadily.
  • Many people only find out their furnace is broken on the first cold night of the year.

Fast Facts

  • Dirty flame sensors are a common cause of furnaces shutting off quickly.
  • An orange or yellow flame can mean improper combustion.
  • Regular tune-ups can catch issues long before the no-heat emergency.

Poll Questions

  • Is the fan running but no heat coming out?
  • Does the pilot light stay on?
  • When was your last furnace tune-up?
Related: AC Not Turning On - High Energy Bills

Tool Idea: "No-Heat Triage Checklist"

The micro-site can walk users through a safe step-by-step triage (no panels removed):

  • Check thermostat mode and temperature.
  • Note any error codes or blinking lights on the furnace.
  • Listen for the sequence: inducer fan -> ignition -> main burner -> blower.

Simple checklist tracker: On the site, users can tap checkboxes to mark what they've already tried and submit that with the lead so the tech arrives pre-briefed.

AC Leaking Water - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/ac-leaking-water or ACLeakingWater.com
Problem Site #3

Overview

Water around the unit can mean a clogged drain line, rusted pan, or frozen coil melting. This site explains why ACs make water in the first place and when to worry about ceilings and flooring.

Trivia

  • High humidity climates can produce several gallons of condensate per day.
  • Some drain lines terminate outside and quietly drip where homeowners never look.
  • Algae and sludge are frequent culprits in drain line clogs.

Fast Facts

  • Water inside near the indoor unit is more urgent than a small drip outside.
  • A safety float switch can shut your system off to prevent overflow.
  • Ignoring small leaks can lead to mold or drywall repairs later.

Poll Questions

  • Is the leak inside or outside your home?
  • Is your AC still cooling properly?
  • Do you see ice on the pipes?

On the real site, this would give a severity label ("urgent if near ceiling or electrical") and nudge the user to schedule service before damage spreads.

Thermostat Not Working - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/thermostat-not-working or ThermostatNotWorking.com
Problem Site #4

Overview

The thermostat is the "cue ball" of the system--every play starts there. This site explains blank screens, short cycling, misread temperatures, and why smart thermostats can help (or hurt if wired wrong).

Trivia

  • Many smart thermostats need a C-wire and can misbehave when power-starved.
  • Placing a thermostat near a lamp or in direct sun can cause phantom temperature swings.
  • Some thermostats can store error history you never knew existed.

Fast Facts

  • Dead batteries can mimic bigger problems--always check them first.
  • Incorrect system type settings can prevent heating or cooling from running.
  • Outdated thermostats may not handle multi-stage or heat pump systems properly.

Poll Questions

  • Is the thermostat screen blank?
  • Do you hear the system click when changing temperature?
  • Would you like a smart thermostat quote?

Weak Airflow & Uneven Rooms - Dedicated Micro-Site

Problem Site #5

Overview

Weak airflow is that subtle annoyance that turns into "Why is the upstairs always miserable?". This micro-site focuses on duct sizing, restrictions, blower problems, and simple fixes like vent balancing.

Trivia

  • Many homes have supply vents closed in too many rooms, choking the system.
  • Furniture and rugs often block returns without homeowners noticing.
  • Leaky ducts in attics can waste conditioned air you already paid for.

Fast Facts

  • Weak airflow can shorten equipment life by stressing motors and coils.
  • Proper duct design is as important as the equipment itself.
  • Sometimes a simple register upgrade can noticeably change comfort.

Poll Questions

  • Is the airflow weak in one room or all rooms?
  • Have your ducts been cleaned in the past two years?
  • Do you notice extra dust in the house?

AC Making Noise - Dedicated Micro-Site

Problem Site #6

Overview

From fan blades hitting branches to compressors groaning on their last summer, this site turns weird sounds into understandable patterns.

Trivia

  • A metallic scraping sound can be as simple as a loose fan shroud.
  • High-pitched squeals often involve belts or bearings.
  • A sudden loud bang can be ductwork flexing with pressure changes.

Fast Facts

  • Ignoring new noises is one of the fastest ways to damage equipment.
  • Recording a short phone video helps techs diagnose before arriving.
  • Some noises are harmless, others are "turn it off now" level.

Poll Questions

  • Is it constant or only when starting?
  • How old is your unit?

AC Freezing Up - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/ac-freezing-up or ACFreezingUp.com
Problem Site #7

Overview

Ice on pipes looks cool until you realize it means your system is suffocating. This site explains filters, airflow, blower speed, and refrigerant from a homeowner's perspective.

Trivia

  • An AC freezing up can eventually flood your drain pan when it thaws.
  • Sometimes just running the fan (no cooling) can help thaw the coil.
  • Low airflow is one of the most common triggers for icing.

Fast Facts

  • Never chip ice off coils--that can damage fins and tubing.
  • Frozen suction lines wrapped in insulation can hide the problem from view.
  • Many freeze-ups involve both airflow and refrigerant issues together.

Poll Questions

  • Do you see ice on your AC lines or coils?
  • When did you last replace the air filter?
  • Does the system run non-stop?

AC Smells Bad - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/ac-smells-bad or ACSmellsBad.com
Problem Site #8

Overview

Smells travel wherever air flows. This site separates musty coil issues from burnt electrical smells and chemical odors from refrigerant leaks or nearby products.

Trivia

  • Dirty sock syndrome is a real nickname for certain coil odor problems.
  • Some smells come from nearby garages or storage areas pulled into returns.
  • Burnt dust odors can be normal for a short time when heat first kicks on.

Fast Facts

  • Persistent musty smells can point to moisture and potential mold.
  • Electrical or "burning plastic" smells should be treated as urgent.
  • UV lights and coil cleaning are common solutions for odor control.

Poll Questions

  • What kind of smell do you notice? (Musty / Burnt / Chemical)
  • Does the smell appear only when AC starts?
  • Do you have pets or recent water damage?

AC Not Turning On - Dedicated Micro-Site
Future URL: https://pooljoe.com/ac-not-turning-on or ACNotTurningOn.com
Problem Site #9

Overview

No clicks, no hum, no airflow--just a house getting warmer. This site covers breakers, disconnects, float switches, thermostat wiring, and when to stop trying to reset things yourself.

Trivia

  • Some outdoor units have a hidden disconnect box that can be accidentally pulled.
  • Condensate float switches can shut the system down to avoid water damage.
  • Weak or failing capacitors are behind a lot of no-start calls.

Fast Facts

  • Repeatedly flipping breakers can be unsafe--there's a reason it keeps tripping.
  • Checking simple things like thermostat batteries can save a service call.
  • Outdoor fans and compressors should be treated with caution--no DIY poking around inside.

Poll Questions

  • Is the thermostat screen on?
  • Do you hear a click when the system should start?
  • Has a breaker tripped recently?

Overview

This micro-site is where math meets comfort. Instead of guessing, homeowners can see how much money old equipment, poor ductwork, or bad habits might be burning.
